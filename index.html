Cats Like Their Space, But That Doesn't Mean They Don't Love You

 


Cats can improve your well-being by providing companionship and reducing loneliness, not to mention giving us a reason to laugh at their playful antics when they have had catnip. They can also help reduce stress and anxiety, which is especially important for people who live alone.

Ragdolls and Birman cats are known for their friendly and affectionate nature, making them great companions for new cat owners. Interacting with animals has been shown to decrease levels of cortisol (a stress-related hormone) and lower blood pressure. Cats can make us feel good and connected since they are more open and curious, and it builds up our capacity for kindness and generosity toward others. Even if they also tend to be independent and introverted, they still form strong bonds with their companions.

Why Cats?

Companionship. Having a cat companion gives you a chance to bond and forge a relationship with another being. This can be especially important for people who live alone and experience anxiety.

As Well as

Increased socialization. For some people, socialization can be a stressful experience, leading to an increase in blood pressure and heart rate. They may expect to be judged by others. With cats, however, people can talk freely, without fearing what other people might think.
